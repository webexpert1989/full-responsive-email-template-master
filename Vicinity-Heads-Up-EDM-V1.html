Google's MAJOR Change to Search Ads

You may or may not have noticed that Google is no longer running ads down the right hand column on desktop computers - and instead will now only show four ads at the top of the Google search results page.

This is a change that has been rolled out globally and will have a few outcomes for you if you are running an AdWords campaign. We've been keeping a close eye on everybody's Google campaign results since the change was made and here is an overview of the main implications we've discovered so far:

Average costs per click will likely be higher, as advertisers are competing for fewer spots on Google.

We're seeing fewer ad impressions for clients who are running limited budgets in competitive industries.

For advertisers who have the budget to bid higher for top 4 positioning, we're seeing stronger click thru rates and better results.

Since the paid ads are now taking up more real estate in Google's search results, we expect to see slightly less visibility and traffic to organic (unpaid) listings for some websites.

We've already been busy making appropriate tweaks to affected clients' campaigns but if you have any questions you can always give Dave or Leena a call.
